# Activation Energy: The Game — Script

> A Nicky-Case-style narrative explainer about willpower, habits, and why you doom-scroll at 2am.

**Voice:** Second-person, casual, punchy. Short sentences. Build to reveals. Let the interactions do the teaching — text sets up, interaction delivers the insight.

**Format:** `[INTERACTION]` blocks describe what the player does. `[VISUAL]` describes what they see. `[TRANSITION]` marks chapter breaks.

---

## Chapter 0: The Morning

It's 6am. You just woke up.

You feel... pretty good, actually. Clear-headed. Ready to do things.

[VISUAL: Character in bed. Willpower bar appears above them, filling to 80. Morning light through window.]

You've got a full tank. 80 units of willpower.

That sounds like a lot. It is.

By 10pm tonight, you'll have 10 units left and you'll be watching your 47th TikTok about a dog that can skateboard.

How does 80 become 10?

Let's find out.

[TRANSITION: Fade to Chapter 1]

## Chapter 1: Starting Energy

Every activity has a price tag. Not in money — in willpower.

To *start* something, you have to pay its Starting Energy. Some things cost a lot. Some cost a little.

And some things... pay *you*.

[INTERACTION: Five activity cards appear: TikTok, Going to Work, Existing Habit, Meditating, The Thing You've Been Avoiding. Player drags each one onto a number line from -50 to +80.]

Go ahead. Where do you think each one goes?

[After player places them, correct positions reveal with animation]

[VISUAL: Cards slide to correct positions. TikTok snaps to -50. The Thing You've Been Avoiding snaps to +80.]

TikTok is at **negative fifty**.

That minus sign is important. It means TikTok doesn't cost willpower to start. TikTok *pulls you in*. You don't choose TikTok. TikTok chooses you.

And that thing you've been putting off? Calling the dentist, filing taxes, having that conversation?

**Eighty.** It costs almost your entire morning tank just to *begin*.

That's not a character flaw. That's physics.

[VISUAL: Character tries to push a boulder labeled "Avoided Thing" up a hill. Versus sliding down a slope labeled "TikTok".]

But here's what nobody tells you: starting energy is only half the equation.

[TRANSITION: Fade to Chapter 2]

---

## Chapter 2: Stopping Energy

Starting things is hard. But *stopping* things? That's where it gets interesting.

Every activity also has Stopping Energy — the willpower cost to *quit* what you're doing and switch to something else.

And Stopping Energy changes over time.

[INTERACTION: Two panels side by side. Left: "Movie." Right: "TikTok." A shared timeline scrubber at the bottom. Player drags the scrubber from 0:30 to 2:30.]

Drag the timeline. Watch what happens.

[VISUAL: Movie stopping energy starts at 35, rises to 40 during the climax, then crashes to near-zero at the credits. TikTok stopping energy starts at 40 and... stays at 40. Maybe dips to 38. Never drops.]

See that?

The movie *ends*. It gives you a natural exit. Credits roll, lights come on, stopping energy drops to zero. You leave.

TikTok never ends. There are no credits. There is no "next episode starts in 5 seconds" countdown. There is only the next video. And the next. And the next.

**TikTok is engineered to never give you a stopping point.**

[VISUAL: Character watching a movie — gets up easily at the end. Character watching TikTok — glued, clock spinning from 10pm to 1am.]

Same thing with casinos. No clocks on the walls. No windows. The stopping energy never drops because they designed it that way.

So here's your equation:

> **Activation Energy = Stopping Energy (current thing) + Starting Energy (new thing)**

To go from TikTok (stopping: 40) to meditating (starting: 20), you need **60 willpower units**.

At 10pm, you have 10.

*That's* why you never meditate at night.

It's not discipline. It's math.

[TRANSITION: Fade to Chapter 3]

---

## Chapter 3: The Day

OK. So starting things costs willpower. Stopping things costs willpower. And you have a limited tank.

What if the *order* matters?

[INTERACTION: Six activity blocks appear: Morning Workout, Breakfast with Family, Deep Work, Lunch Break TikTok, Afternoon Meetings, Evening Gym. Player drags them into a day timeline from 6am to 10pm.]

Plan a day. Drag the blocks into order.

[After placing, simulation runs]

[VISUAL: Character moves through the day. Willpower bar drains and (sometimes) recharges at each transition. Activation energy costs appear as the character switches between activities.]

Now watch.

[VISUAL: If player put morning workout first, willpower actually *increases* briefly — morning habits boost the bar from 80 to 90. If they put TikTok at lunch, willpower crashes and afternoon activities become very expensive. The character visibly struggles.]

[INTERACTION: Player can rearrange and re-run. A/B comparison: "Try putting the workout in the morning vs. the evening. Try removing TikTok. Try adding it."]

Did you catch it?

Morning habits don't just *cost* willpower — they *generate* it. Your workout fires up your body. Journaling settles your mind. Family breakfast fills your emotional tank.

You went in with 80 and came out with 90.

But that TikTok at lunch? You went in with 60 and came out with 35. And you lost 25 more minutes because the stopping energy kept you trapped.

**The same activities in different order produce completely different days.**

This is why morning routines aren't a productivity hack. They're an *energy strategy*.

[VISUAL: Two timelines side by side. "Optimized Day" — character is energized through evening. "Default Day" — character is cooked by 3pm.]

But we've been lying to you a little. That willpower bar? It's not actually one bar.

[TRANSITION: Fade to Chapter 4]

## Chapter 4: The Fibers

Remember that willpower bar?

[VISUAL: The single bar appears]

It's actually this.

[VISUAL: The bar splits into 5 colored strands braided together, like a rope. Each strand is labeled: Professional (blue), Physical (green), Emotional (purple), Family (orange), Creative (yellow).]

Your willpower isn't one muscle. It's five.

Think of it like a group of people lifting a heavy log. When everyone lifts together, the log rises easily. When one person drops out...

[INTERACTION: Five characters hold up a log together. Player clicks one to make them let go.]

[VISUAL: The log drops slightly. Remaining four strain harder. The activation energy threshold line stays the same but the total willpower drops. Some activities that were "affordable" are now above the line.]

*Click.*

[Player removes another character]

[VISUAL: Log drops more. The three remaining characters are visibly struggling. More activities become unaffordable. The gym is now above the willpower line.]

That's why a breakup makes it impossible to focus at work. Why losing your job makes you stop exercising. Why skipping the gym makes you snap at your kids.

**Every role in your life is a willpower fiber. When one breaks, the others have to carry the weight.**

[INTERACTION: Five sliders, one per fiber. Player can weaken/strengthen each one. A radar chart shows total willpower shape. Activities light up green (affordable) or red (too expensive) based on total willpower.]

Try it. What happens when you take a gap year? (Professional → 0)

[VISUAL: Total willpower drops from 85 to 50. Gym activation energy (40) is now barely affordable. Creative work (35) is borderline. And The Thing You've Been Avoiding (80)? Forget about it.]

What about when you're sick for a week? (Physical → 0)

[VISUAL: Different collapse pattern. Brain fog hits everything. Emotional regulation weakens. Even "going to work" now costs more than it should.]

Every fiber affects every other fiber. This isn't five independent bars. It's one system.

And when one fiber starts pulling the others down...

[TRANSITION: Fade to Chapter 5]

## Chapter 5: The Death Spiral

Let's watch a spiral happen.

[INTERACTION: A "Play" button. When pressed, an animation begins.]

[VISUAL: Character starts with all 5 fibers healthy. Day 1: Stays up late (TikTok). Physical fiber weakens slightly.]

Day 1: You stay up late scrolling. No big deal. Everyone does it sometimes.

[VISUAL: Day 2: Tired. Physical fiber weaker. Emotional fiber starts to dip — less patience. Character skips workout.]

Day 2: You're tired. Skip the workout. Snap at a coworker. Eat junk for lunch because you don't have the willpower to choose something better.

[VISUAL: Day 3: Physical and emotional fibers both weak. Professional fiber starts to dip — can't focus. Character reaches for phone more often.]

Day 3: Can't focus. The phone keeps appearing in your hand. You don't remember picking it up.

[VISUAL: Day 4-5: Cascade accelerates. Family fiber weakens (short-tempered at home). Creative fiber dies (no energy for anything beyond survival). The rope is fraying visibly.]

Day 5: All five fibers are weakened. Your total willpower is 25. The only thing with activation energy low enough to start is...

[VISUAL: TikTok card glowing. Starting energy: -50. It's the only thing you CAN do.]

...TikTok. Which makes everything worse. Which makes TikTok easier. Which makes everything worse.

**That's a death spiral.** One weakened fiber cascading into all five, until the only affordable activity is the one destroying you.

[INTERACTION: "Rewind" button. Player can restart the spiral from any day and make a different choice.]

But here's the thing: it also works in reverse.

[VISUAL: Player rewinds to Day 2. This time, character forces the workout (costs most of their willpower for the morning). Physical fiber recovers. Emotional fiber stabilizes. By Day 3, the spiral breaks.]

One good choice doesn't fix everything. But it stops the cascade.

[TRANSITION: Fade to Chapter 6]

## Chapter 6: The Levers

So you've got five fibers, a limited tank, and a world designed to drain you.

What can you actually do?

Three levers.

### Lever 1: Morning Habits

[INTERACTION: Toggle "Morning Habits" ON/OFF. Watch the willpower curve for an entire day change.]

Morning habits don't add willpower. They *activate all five fibers at once*.

- Exercise → Physical fiber fires up
- Journaling → Emotional + Creative fibers engage
- Family breakfast → Family fiber warms up
- Review your plan → Professional fiber pre-loads

[VISUAL: With morning habits ON, the willpower curve starts at 90 instead of 70, and decays slower. More activities stay "affordable" through the evening.]

It's like a warm-up before lifting. You're not stronger — you're more *coordinated*.

### Lever 2: Schedules

[INTERACTION: Two versions of the same day. Left: "I should probably go to the gym... maybe at 3? Or after work? Let me think about it..." Right: "Gym is at 6:30am. That's when I go." Player watches both play out.]

Every choice is a willpower leak.

"Should I go to the gym?" costs willpower. "When should I go?" costs willpower. "What should I wear?" costs willpower.

A schedule eliminates all those choices. The gym isn't a decision — it's a fact. Like gravity.

[VISUAL: Left character agonizes, eventually skips gym. Right character just... goes. No deliberation. No willpower spent on the decision itself.]

The calendar doesn't add willpower. It stops you from *wasting* it on choices.

### Lever 3: Peers

[INTERACTION: Character surrounded by 5 peer characters. Player adjusts peer willpower levels. Watch how the character's behavior shifts.]

You become the average of the people around you.

[VISUAL: With high-willpower peers, character's activities shift toward productive ones. With low-willpower peers, character's activities shift toward low-cost/addictive ones. The effect is magnetic — peers literally pull your willpower up or down.]

Your peers don't give you willpower. They change what feels *normal*. When everyone around you is working out, not working out feels weird. When everyone's scrolling, not scrolling feels weird.

[VISUAL: Peer gravity visualization. Your willpower naturally drifts toward the group average.]

## Chapter 7: Your Day (Sandbox)

You know the system now.

- Activities have starting and stopping costs
- Willpower is five fibers, not one bar
- Order matters — morning habits supercharge everything
- Schedules eliminate choice leaks
- Peers shift your gravity

[INTERACTION: Full sandbox. Player has:
- All activities (draggable into timeline)
- Fiber health sliders (simulate life events)
- Three lever toggles (morning habits, schedules, peers)
- Real-time willpower curve
- Activation energy budget showing what's affordable at each hour]

Design your ideal day. Then break it — weaken a fiber, remove a lever — and see what survives.

[VISUAL: Player's designed day runs as a full animation. Character lives through it. Willpower bar + fiber breakdown visible at all times.]

The goal isn't to have perfect willpower. The goal is to understand the *system* well enough to work with it instead of against it.

You don't need more discipline.

You need better physics.

[END]

## Script Notes

**Total word count:** ~1,500 (Nicky Case's Evolution of Trust is ~3,300 words). This is deliberately tighter — the interactions carry more weight than text.

**Key design principles:**
1. **Text sets up, interaction delivers.** Never explain what the player can discover.
2. **Each chapter has exactly one "aha" moment.** Don't dilute.
3. **Progressive complexity.** Ch1-2 are single concepts. Ch3 combines them. Ch4-5 add the fiber model. Ch6-7 give control.
4. **The sandwich.** Narrative text → interaction → narrative reaction to what just happened.
5. **Second person, present tense, always.** "You wake up." Not "The player wakes up."

**Character notes:**
- Simple, expressive. Think XKCD stick figures or Nicky Case's round characters, not realistic.
- Willpower bar should be prominent and always visible — it's the emotional anchor.
- Fiber colors should be consistent and distinct throughout.

**Sound design:**
- Minimal. Soft chimes for transitions. A satisfying "click" when dragging activities. A descending tone when willpower drops. An ascending tone when it rises.
- Death spiral chapter should have increasing tension — maybe a heartbeat that speeds up.
- NO background music. Let silence do the work.
